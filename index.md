<h3><strong>There is no God</strong></h3>

<p>Years ago I read a statement “There is no God, only Godliness” by Osho. It hit me so deep, so hard that the question of God disappeared in me forever.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>It’s my view that the simplest explanation is that there is no God. No one created the universe and no one directs our fate. This leads me to a profound realisation: there is probably no heaven and afterlife either. I think belief in an afterlife is just wishful thinking. <a href="https://www.brainpickings.org/2019/07/17/stephen-hawking-brief-answers-to-the-big-questions/">Is There a God? Stephen Hawking Gives the Definitive Answer to the Eternal Question</a></p>
</blockquote>

<h3><strong>Sleep Industry</strong></h3>

<p>First they robbed our sleep, now they are coming to sell it back to us.</p>

<blockquote>
<p> The sleep economy is growing in India. More devices are being created or entering the market. But by far the biggest competition is in the mattress business. A number of startups, some of them venture capital-funded, have entered this space in the last few years. They call themselves sleep solution companies. <a href="https://openthemagazine.com/features/the-sleep-economy/">The Sleep Economy</a></p>
</blockquote>

<h3><strong>Crisis of Teachers</strong></h3>

<p>Our higher education system is in tatters. Instead of fixing the fundamentals we are trying to improve metrics through technology.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>So the fundamental question is that, are we concerned about the utilisation of digital technology in education or are we more convinced for digitisation of the entire education system? <a href="https://countercurrents.org/2020/01/the-existential-crisis-of-teachers-in-the-age-of-technology">The Existential Crisis of Teachers in the Age of Technology!</a></p>
</blockquote>

<h3><strong>End of an Idea</strong></h3>

<p>Turning India into a macho, muscle flexing, ultra-nationalist, openly Hindu country does not automatically makes it a benevolent, moral and ethical state. As long there is injustice, human spirit continues to challenge power.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>In practice, India has been neither secular nor socialist. It has always functioned as an upper-caste Hindu state. But the conceit of secularism, hypocritical though it may be, is the only shard of coherence that makes India possible. That hypocrisy was the best thing we had. Without it, India will end. <a href="https://www.thenation.com/article/arundhati-roy-assam-modi/">India: Intimations of an Ending</a> #longread</p>
</blockquote>
